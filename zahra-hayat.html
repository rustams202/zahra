About

I am an Assistant Professor of Anthropology at the University of British Columbia and a Harvard Academy Postdoctoral Fellow at the Harvard Academy for International and Area Studies (2024–26). I work at the intersection of medical anthropology and law, studying pharmaceutical capitalism, intellectual property, and narcotics control.

My work is driven by the reality of preventable death and suffering in the Global South. These stakes shape the arc of my career in both academia and legal advocacy — from representing asylum applicants, to advocating for healthcare for children in foster care, to highlighting the persecution of religious minorities in Pakistan under blasphemy laws.

I obtained my PhD in Anthropology from UC Berkeley in 2022. Before my doctoral program, I practiced law in the San Francisco Bay Area for five years — first as an advocate for children in foster care at the National Center for Youth Law, then as an intellectual property litigator at Morrison & Foerster. My first law degree, the BA in Jurisprudence, is from Oxford University, where I was a Rhodes Scholar. I also hold an LLM from Yale Law School.

Research

Scandal in a Spectral Place
Book Manuscript · In Progress

Scandal in a Spectral Place: Pharmaceutical Capitalism in Pakistan

Pakistan has some of the world's lowest drug prices — and almost no patents filed by Western multinationals. Yet it is burdened by severe shortages of life-saving drugs and a devastating epidemic of unpalliated cancer pain due to morphine scarcity.

This book unravels these paradoxes by rethinking the relationships between pharmaceutical access and price, property, and narcotics control. It draws on two years of ethnographic fieldwork in Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad, and foregrounds the geopolitical entanglements of pharmaceutical political economies.

🏆 S.S. Pirzada Prize — Best Dissertation on Pakistan, 2023

Pharmaceutical Capitalism

How global pricing regimes, IP law, and narcotics control shape who lives and who dies across the Global South.

Intellectual Property & Law

The role of IP frameworks — and their surprising absence — in pharmaceutical access in countries like Pakistan.

Regimes of Pain

How militarized narcotics control and price ceilings make cheap, effective opioids like morphine scarce in hospitals while heroin proliferates illicitly.

Teaching

My courses foreground the deep — and under-explored — relationships between medical anthropology and law. I teach across genre: film, fiction, and legal decisions sit alongside ethnography in my syllabi.

ANTH — New Course

The Capitalist Lives of Pharmaceuticals

Examines how the calculi of Big Pharma condition pharmaceutical access globally, from patent cliffs to parallel trade to compulsory licensing.

ANTH — New Course

Medical Anthropology and Law

Trains a feminist legal lens on the fraught ethical terrains of abortion, genetics, clinical trials, and opioid addiction.

ANTH — Core

South Asian Societies

Introductory and advanced courses on South Asia — history, politics, religion, and contemporary issues including blasphemy law and healthcare.

ANTH — Interdisciplinary

As an inaugural member of the IP, Technology & Justice Research Group at UBC's Allard Law School, I bridge law and anthropology in classroom and research alike.
